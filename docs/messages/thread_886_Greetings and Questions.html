From: Hedglin, Nils A (Nils.A.Hedglin@Intel.Com) | Date: 2005-09-15 08:46:38 UTC-07:00
Hi & welcome,
I don't know if you're a member of the Letterboxing USA yahoo group,
in the group's File folder is an overly comprehensive list of what
people bring:
http://f1.grp.yahoofs.com/v1/cIwpQ2VdtdtU8xdmTbWd4x7PZk-1bKrS-zIRtMZYLit
ap9U7clQ_Qxp_fV0KKuF1GX63PDPG6MGm86TXq7qPghCPGU5pepZ2rQiN9NKxgWv82L8OOME
/What%27s%20in%20your%20kit%3F

Other things that I bring that haven't been mentioned yet are extra
baggies to replace ones in a letterbox that have holes in them, a
compass, an printed map of the area (usually from one of the online map
sites), sunscreen & a hat depending on how long you'll be out. My wife
keeps nagging me to bring a 1st aid kit, especially after being stung by
a bee yesterday while boxing. & something that I should have brought
are extra logbooks since 2 of the boxes we found had full logbooks.

A warning that you may have seen already about baby-type wipes, most of
them are scented in some way & if you use them to clean a stamp, the
scent will remain & may be attractive to animals that may dig the box
up. It's usually recommended to only use water & paper napkin to clean
the stamps so there's no residual scent on it.

Have fun & be sure to ask any other questions you have,

Black Cavalier

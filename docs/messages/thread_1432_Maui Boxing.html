We went boxing in Maui a few months ago. Funhog's Maui Sunset is easy
to reach and offers a lovely view. Canoes is cute and Lindberg's Grave
puts an exclamation point on the drive to Hana.

We went after a two box series by Funhog (one stamp by Legerdemaine).
Had a fabulous hike in the high forest, but found neither box. There
were a few other boxes that were fun to find, but nothing special
except the enjoyment of boxing on Maui.

Get a tan and enjoy the soothing surf.

From: funhog1 (funhog1@yahoo.com) | Date: 2007-03-01 07:58:02 UTC
Ah...lovely Maui. I just left there yesterday. While I was on the
island, I too stumbled over that hot lava field. The King's Highway
box is in it's little hidey hole safe and sound awaiting visitors. The
black sand beach nearby is as lovely (and deserted) as ever. I spent a
good portion of the afternoon there without seeing another soul and
this on Maui in high season!

The Maui Sunset box is in place and has been visited many times. The
two boxes in the high forest were another story. I intended to check
those in the morning and hit the lava fields in the afternoon. Sadly,
recent forest fires have resulted in the closing of all trails in the
Poli Poli Reserve. Not only that, but the road is gated. The area will
reopen in May after extensive cleanup but there's a good chance that
the letterboxes there are toast, literally. I won't be back until next
year but be forewarned. Manu Make may be truly Make. Funhog

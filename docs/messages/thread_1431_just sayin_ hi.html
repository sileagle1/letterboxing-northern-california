I was getting these posts sent directly to my email, but haven't seen

I crashed my car bigtime on the Sunday after Thanksgiving, coming back
from visiting my sister in Salinas. I took a detour on the way
home--to Oakhurst for, um, boxes--and that delayed me enough that I
didn't miss the storm as I thought I would. On Hwy 88 about 30 miles
above Jackson, a guy in a big old pickup lost control and spun into my
lane. He wasn't moving much any more, but I only had time to decide I
didn't want to slam on my brakes or drive off the road before I knew I
was going to crash into him. Funky. Just know that there's a reason
Subaru has a five star safety rating; it was a walk-away. Digger was
with me and he was completely unhurt. I had some bumps and bruises and
am still healing a ligament injury in my ribs, but otherwise, I am
fine. Everyone I dealt with was absolutely great, and I thank my car
for giving its life for me. I actually have a long list of things to
be grateful for in this experience. As Carole Kat said, that's kind of
a tough way to get a new car. And it is, but I'm enjoying the new
Outback now. Haven't put more than local miles on it yet (still lying
a little low), but the time will come.

I hope you're all doing well...can't make it for group boxing at the

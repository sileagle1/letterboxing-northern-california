why don't you all live in upstate New York?

From: Lisa Lazar (lazar.bauer@earthlink.net) | Date: 2005-05-05 04:46:40 UTC
I think I've mentioned that I spend every summer painting
scenery for an opera company in Cooperstown, New York. I've
been happily carving stamps with the notion of stashing them in
all of my favorite places.

I just finished a large stamp in honor of the amazing artists at
Adelphi Paperhanging (which despite what the website says,
has its studio in a tiny town called Sharon Springs NY). Adelphi
is the Holy Grail of relief printing. They hand print period
reproduction wallpaper with wooden blocks.

http://www.adelphipaperhangings.com/unique.html

I have been torturing myself a bit, because there don't seem to
be any active letterboxers near me. Near is a pretty relative term,
as this is a very rural area. I want to invite you all over to my
house to see this baby before I send my stamp out into the wild
world.

And while I'm torturing myself, can someone advise me on how
well Speedy Stamp holds up to very, very cold weather. Weeks
on end in the single digits? I'm thinking of asking the folks at
Adelphi to keep an eye on the stamp, but I don't want to be a
burden on them, by making this a stamp that you ask a business
owner about. The studio has a big store-front window, and they
allow gawkers, but are not open for tours.

Thanks for putting up with my excessive zealousness.

Lisascenic

From: Lea Shangraw Fox (princesslea@alamedanet.net) | Date: 2005-05-05 09:22:18 UTC-07:00

On May 4, 2005, at 9:46 PM, Lisa Lazar wrote:

> And while I'm torturing myself, can someone advise me on how
> well Speedy Stamp holds up to very, very cold weather. Weeks
> on end in the single digits?

I have done some boxing in Vermont where it can get down to 35 below
zero. I was AMAZED at how well the stamps held up, and even the crummy
GladWare containers were okay. Last summer I found some stamps that
had been hidden in 1998 and they were as good as new. Problems arise
more from infiltrating water than the cold - in fact, I'd say the cold
may even preserve the box! The Speedy Stamp is the pink stuff, right?
That stuff is indestructible.

-- Princess Lea

Hi, I'm assuming it's the Crissy Field just east of the Golden Gate?
Here is a link to the park map, the center is just south of the Tidal
Marsh.
http://www.crissyfield.org/pdfs/crissymap.pdf

Also, in case you might be interested in the art exhibit, here's some
info on it:

Celebrate 'Katrina's Children' Opening Event
Saturday, January 28, 2:30 to 4 p.m.

Co-founded by former US Poet Laureate Robert Hass and East Bay writer
Pamela Michael, River of Words has assembled an unparalleled collection
of Gulf Coast children's art and poetry. Entitled 'Katrina's Children,'
the exhibit will be on display in the Center's Gathering Room. This
display will also feature interpretive panels that consider the
connections between loss of wetlands and storm destruction, so
tragically illustrated in New Orleans and the rest of the Gulf Coast in
2005.

Join us for an inspiring day of art, poetry, science, and song at Crissy
Field Center. In addition to viewing the 'Katrina's Children' exhibit,
get a little messy with hands-on activities that show how wetlands
protect us, and learn how you can get involved in wetlands restoration.
You';ll also be able to listen to young poets read their work and enjoy
live Cajun music, all while digging into the celebration cake that will
be on hand to mark River of Words 10th anniversary.

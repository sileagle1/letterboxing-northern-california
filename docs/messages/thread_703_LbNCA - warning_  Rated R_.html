LbNCA - warning! Rated R!

From: Lea Shangraw Fox (princesslea@alamedanet.net) | Date: 2005-05-02 16:31:04 UTC-07:00
I need to add my thanks onto the pile for the organizers of the GRR.
Having been on the planning committees for the last 2 events, I know
how much energy it requires - I was *so glad* to just be an attendee
this time around! Thanks also to DoubleSaj and Old Blue for letting
me, Amanda and Ryan crash in Davis the night before.

Amanda and I had a little post adventure on Sunday we wanted to share.
We went up to do the Greatest Liar series in the Oakland Hills before
Amanda had to return to work. All was going well until we got to the
last box which was hidden in a stone tower. This monument built by
Joaquin Miller is probably about 20 feet tall, and is round. You step
up into a little room that is about 3 feet off the ground and about 4
feet in diameter, and then you are "inside" the tower, but there's a
big open "doorway" facing outward. Anyway, the poor letterbox was
lying in pieces on the floor of this little room (note to Alien
Explorers - I picked up the pieces, but the logbook and stamp were gone
- can return it to you in due time). Bummer. Amanda and I decided to
go explore the Woodminster Amphitheater which is about 100 feet away.
As we are coming back down the path from this side trip, we can see
through some trees that a couple of people have entered the monument
where the letterbox was supposed to be hidden. So Amanda and I are
intently peering at them, wondering if they are letterboxers. Here is
some of the conversation that ensues (beware - this is where things go
downhill to the R rating):

"Hey are they looking for the letterbox?"
"Do they have clues in their hands?"
"The lady just put her head out the door - she has long blond hair,
maybe it's Mimulus!"
"Wait, why is she getting down on all fours?"
"Is there someone standing behind her?"
"Wait a minute, she's not wearing her pants anymore! OMIGOD THEY ARE
DOING IT IN THERE!"

We were completely traumatized. Now we have a new answer for our
AtlasQuest interview under the questions What's the worst injury or
hardship you've suffered while tracking down a box? or What's the most
bizarre thing you've ever discovered while letterboxing? The worst
thing is that after we slinked away after our episode of voyeurism, we
walked 50 feet to where there were several families with small children
exploring a nearby waterfall. Is this some new trend we should know
about - having sex at the sites of really good letterboxes to
commemorate the event? Guess we should be glad we didn't come along
looking for the letterbox while they were actually in there fooling
around!

-- Princess Lea

From: Hunter (h.m.currey@gmail.com) | Date: 2007-05-07 00:08:22 UTC
Today we planted the Bay Area Bug hitch hiker.

It is a low impact hitch hiker. When you find it, I don't want you to
curse the letterboxing heavens that you found another *^$%@ hitch
hiker; there is no accompanying logbook, and no obligation to stamp
all over the place. Just stamp, date (or not), and leave it. Don't
worry about writing that you left it, or writing any little notes, or
anything. Hitch hikers are supposed to be fun.

To make it more fun, the Bay Area Bug is for the San Francisco Bay
Area only. This way, we'll see its little stamp all over. The exact
range for the Bay Area Bug is up for debate. I kind of had it imagined
as anywhere in the LBNCA area, which is bascially northern California.
There are plenty of boxes to keep it busy for a long time!

If you have it, and it's too big to fit into a letterbox, stamp it in
anyway just to have it say hi (or not)!

Hope to see it moving around!

Westwood Hills Regional Park Boxes

From: Lynnette Seymour (llseymour@hotmail.com) | Date: 2004-10-04 21:10:08 UTC

We had a great time on Saturday, we did the Westwood Hills Regional
Park Boxes in Napa. We found them both fairly easily and enjoyed the
location immensely. Fabulous! Both were well hidden when we found
them and we made sure we left them like that.

We were laughing at the log book in which someone mentions there was
a snake living in the hole! Great, tell us after we get the box out
of the hole! Actually there were no problems and there was very
little poison oak to worry about as well.

We went with my sister and brother-in-law and kids. They are geo-
cachers just starting with letterboxing. We are (new-ish) boxers and
recent cache-converts too.

So for those of you who like to do both boxing and cacheing (I call
it geo-boxing), this is a fantastic location. We did two of each.

Sunday, I spent the day carving stamps. So it was a great-all-around
boxing weekend.

A postal letterbox is a letterbox that is Mailed (via snail mail)
from person to person. Usually the person who creates the Postal
Letterbox compiles a list of people who would like the box. Then the
box is mailed from one person to the next on the list.

Some boxes have specific themes--A Postal Letterbox for Moms, A
Postal Letterbox trying to get to all 50 states, A Postal Letterbox
for everyone who has found 100 letterboxes etc.

Usually the person who creates the box will annouce on one of the
Yahoo Groups Boards information about the letterbox and ask if
anybody would like to be included in the mailing list. Once you are
part of the list, it is your responsibility to keep the Postal
Letterbox moving by stamping it and sending it on to the next person
as soon as possible.

--Amanda from Seattle

From: (Doublesaj@aol.com) | Date: 2004-11-14 19:43:31 UTC-05:00
Go to www.groups@yahoo.com and search for the postal_letterboxing group.
Then just sign up for it and you'll get all the opportunities your
postage-paying fingers can handle!

Be forewarned though that many of these boxes are exclusive to certain
people. There's a box for people who were born in October (the Libra Box) another
which insists you try a recipe inside the box before you send it back out, and
still another that made you write a story about a summer vacation you remember
as a child before they'd add you to the list. Also, each box is usually
restricted to the first 25 or 35 or 50 respondents. It's not always easy to get
into each box unless you read the email every day.

Each and every box is NOT for every letterboxer.

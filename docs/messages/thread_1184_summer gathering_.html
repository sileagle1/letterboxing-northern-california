From: (Doublesaj@aol.com) | Date: 2006-05-25 22:19:01 UTC-04:00
We're always up for a gathering and would love to help too. Personally, we
think it's nice to find new places for gatherings rather than places we've
already scoured. Might we suggest one of the wonderful regional parks we've
recently discovered because of letterboxing.

Within the past few weeks, we've 'boxed in 3 regional parks in the Bay
Area--Redwood Regional Park in Oakland where Princess Lea has a box and Coyote
Hills Regional Park in Fremont where Lisascenic has a series and Black Diamond
Regional Park in Antioch where there are several really nice boxes. Each of
these parks has a perfect picnic area for a Letterboxing Gathering.

Other than picking a location, which, I think is the easy part, the thing
that always take a while is selecting a date. Why don't we start with a month.
How about August?

From: Lea Shangraw Fox (princesslea@alamedanet.net) | Date: 2006-05-26 19:53:30 UTC
I like the idea of another gathering (I think we are due one since that last one was Old Sac
in April 2005) - I believe there were rumblings about doing one in Fairfield or the North
Bay but I haven't heard anything more. I'm all for doing the East Bay again (eg Redwood
Park as DS&OB suggest) but that would be selfishly motivated. Coyote Hills doesn't really
offer as many hiding spots as Redwood. The other idea I had was Monterey as there is a
good size population of boxes down there. Ultimately it will depend on who wants to take
this ball and run with it! August is good for me.

Most of the boxes in GGP are vestiges of the 2004 event (although quite a few of them are
gone). Paul in SF clarified at the time that only the shoreline area is considered part of the
NPS so it's not all national land. EIther way, most of us were fairly oblique about the
location in our clues.

-- Princess Lea

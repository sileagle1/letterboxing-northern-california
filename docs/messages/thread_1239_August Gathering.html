From: Lisa Lazar (lazar.bauer@earthlink.net) | Date: 2006-07-03 21:57:26 UTC
To clarify my comment about the Middle harbor Park being "wide
open," what I meant is that this type of park requires thinking like
a stealthy urban letterboxer, because there are fewer spots
forhiding big honkin' tupperware boxes. I saw *lots* of great
hiding places. In fact, I was delighted at the options. I think
that teh Sacramento gathering offered the same sort of challenges,
albeit on a larger scale.

BUT TO BE TOTALLY SELFISH....

I have to cast my vote for the Middle Harbor Park.

While the picnic site at Redwood is accessible, the rest of that
park isn't. So that means that Robb would be stuck at the picnic
table during the entire gathering. Not terribly interesting for him.

The flat terrain and paved trails at Middle Harbor provide Robb the
ability to participate in this event, and not be left sitting on his
butt while we all run around.

I really don't think the park will be too hot. Quite the contrary.
There was a stiff bay breeze blowing the day we were there. I think
this could be cooler than the hills.

This is, of course, just my preference.

Lisa

ps -- Lock Wench has offered a letterbox that relates to the Port of
Oakland. If you know her work, this is a pretty big incentive!

From: pediamom (pediamom@yahoo.com) | Date: 2006-07-04 01:19:17 UTC
I've been mostly lurking in regards to this event, but I just gotta
speak up. I think for a gathering, the most important thing is the
company of our fellow boxers and the enjoyment of the event for each
and every one of us. I don't think that the permanence of boxes
should be the overriding priority-- Permanent boxes can be placed
any old time and if there is great concern, the event boxes can be
temporary and replaced in a safer place after the event (like the
one last year!)

I think that we should try hard to be accomodating here and make it
fun for our less mobile parties, get creative, and do this shindig
at a place where there will be fun for all (sitting at a picnic site
while everyone else is out hunting is so NOT fun) especially to give
access to some of the locals who have made the East Bay one of the
most creative, fun places to box in the Bay area---

My vote: Call it anything you want, but let's consider Redwood
Regional for another time and do Shoreline Park this year!!

Anna

From: Lea Shangraw Fox (princesslea@alamedanet.net) | Date: 2006-07-05 17:12:57 UTC
I hope that no one interprets my pro-Redwood sentiments as anti-Robb in any way. I was
involved in the first Northern California gathering (at Kennedy Grove) and at that time,
there was a dearth of boxes around here, so the planting of permanent boxes *was* the
goal of the event. Now that we have evolved into a more letterbox-rich area, maybe that it
not as important anymore. So, if your goal is to socialize then that could affect the kind of
venue you would choose - it's just a matter of preference.

Regardless of the venue, I would like to urge restraint when it comes to cooties! I got to
learn a lot about these critters up at Stargazers, notably about how people become
compulsive about collecting them all (I was guilty of this). Their original intent was to
amuse the children so the so-called grown-ups could concentrate on exchanges and
boxing without interference. I heard a rumor that there was a series of cooties at the NY
gathering that represented the alphabet - so I guess there were 26 of them. That's a bit
much! So, cooties are cool, but too many cooties gets to be a bit annoying.

-- Princess Lea

From: jceg5540 (greendoc@sonic.net) | Date: 2006-08-04 13:55:38 UTC
Here it is cut and pated from the national chat list:


La-dies andd Gen-tle-men,

Spring has sprung and so has Boch's Circus from winter hibernation.

I went down to the train station yesterday. Amidst the excitement of
the busy platform I waved "bon show" to the departing circus train.
The ears of the Dancing Dog flapped in the wind as it hung out the
window. Boch's Traveling Circus and Wild Menagerie is back on the
road for a new season of regaling an appreciative audience with some
of the finest impressions of physical entertainment from around the
country. ["And more blather from his Ringmaster
Verboseness." "Hushh."] As I was saying ...

Upcoming performances include:

-Ice Cream Sundae Letterboxing Event - Carlisle, Massachusetts -
April 23, 2006

---A sampling half dozen of some of our finest and fun acts.

-Wilmington Film Fest - Wilmington, North Carolina - May 13, 2006

---At the Film Fest we plan a rambunkxious Circus Clown reunion when
a new act joins us. Introduced by his great grand neice, Bert Leo
shall make a triumphal return to the "great show" and meet Boch's
other circus clownsters. (We have more clowns than I even realized.)
Yes his real great grand niece will attend and has graciously agreed
to exchange and sign autographs.

Many other new acts joined us in our last travels and are available
for future performances:

- That master string puller The Puppet Master - by Springchick
- The unbelieveable strong man Maciste Potenti - by Azono Boxeretti
- A death defying High Wire Tight Rope Walker - by Phyto
- The mesmerizing Plate Spinner - by She Who Plants
- The stunningly beautiful and mysterious Bird of Paradise - by
Carte Blanche
- Rumor has it that a Jumping Jack has run away with the circus. -
by Springchick (Shh. I don't think he wants her to know.)

These acts and more are available for bookings at your local
gathers.

Onward with the shows.

Most perspicaciously yours,
Phineas Boch
Ringmaster and Producer
Bochs Circus and Wild Menagerie
Bailey's Crossroads, Virginia
